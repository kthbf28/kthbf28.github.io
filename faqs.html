General Pressure Washing Questions

What is pressure washing, and how is it different from soft washing?

Pressure washing uses high-pressure water to remove dirt, grime, mold, and stains from hard surfaces. Soft washing uses low pressure combined with specialized cleaning solutions and is safer for more delicate surfaces like siding and roofs.

How often should I have my property pressure washed?

Most homes benefit from pressure washing once a year. Properties with heavy shade, moisture, or pollen buildup may require more frequent cleanings.

How long does a pressure washing job take?

Most residential pressure washing jobs take between 1–3 hours, depending on the size of the property and the surfaces being cleaned.

Do I need to be home during the service?

No, as long as we have access to the areas being cleaned and a working water source, you do not need to be present.

What Can and Cannot Be Pressure Washed

What surfaces can be pressure washed?

Common surfaces that can be safely pressure washed include:

  • Driveways and sidewalks
  • Concrete and pavers
  • Brick and stone
  • Decks (wood or composite, using proper pressure)
  • Patios and pool decks
  • Vinyl, aluminum, and fiber cement siding
  • Fences
  • Garage floors
What surfaces should NOT be pressure washed?

Some materials can be damaged by high pressure and require soft washing or alternative cleaning methods:

  • Asphalt shingle roofs
  • Old, weathered, or damaged wood
  • Stucco (depending on condition)
  • Painted surfaces with peeling or flaking paint
  • Windows and window screens
  • Outdoor light fixtures
Can pressure washing damage my property?

When done incorrectly, pressure washing can cause damage. Professional pressure washing uses proper equipment, correct pressure levels, and surface-specific techniques to clean safely and effectively.

Cleaning Results & Expectations

Will pressure washing remove all stains?

Most dirt, mold, mildew, and algae can be fully removed. Some deep stains such as oil or rust may significantly lighten but not completely disappear, depending on how long they have set.

How long do the results last?

Results typically last between 6–12 months or longer, depending on weather conditions, shade, landscaping, and surface type.

Do you use chemicals or detergents?

Yes, when necessary. We use professional-grade, surface-safe, and environmentally responsible cleaning solutions to enhance results while minimizing pressure.

Safety & Preparation

Is pressure washing safe for plants and landscaping?

Yes. Plants are typically pre-wet and rinsed after cleaning to protect landscaping from cleaning solutions.

What should I do to prepare for my appointment?

Customers are generally asked to:

  • Move vehicles away from the work area
  • Secure pets indoors
  • Remove fragile outdoor items
  • Ensure access to a water source
Is pressure washing environmentally friendly?

Professional pressure washing uses less water than prolonged hose use and relies on biodegradable cleaners when needed.
